Key Findings & Insights
Data-driven conclusions from the Medicaid beneficiary dataset
🏥
Low Utilization Problem

41.2% of all beneficiaries had zero doctor visits. AFDC program has a much higher zero-visit rate (49.7%) vs SSI (33.1%).

👴
Age Drives Utilization

80+ year olds average 2.73 visits vs just 0.94 for teenagers. SSI skews elderly (avg 78 years) explaining its higher utilization.

🔬
Health1 Strongest Predictor

Physical health score (r=0.27) is the strongest correlate with visits — far above income or age individually.

♂️
Male Gender Effect

Unmarried males average 2.47 visits vs 1.77 for married males. Marriage appears protective for male utilization.

📉
Children Inverse Effect

More children = fewer doctor visits. 0-children avg: 2.28 visits; 5+ children avg: 0.76 visits — possibly caregiver time constraints.

🎓
Education Non-Linear

Grade 12 is modal (213 people). Education has near-zero correlation with visits (r=0.01), suggesting it's not a utilization driver.
